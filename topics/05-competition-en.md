## 1. RoboCup 2026

### Overview

RoboCup (Robot World Cup) is the world's largest and most influential robot soccer and rescue competition, often called the "Robot World Cup." Founded in 1997, its mission is to advance AI and robotics through soccer, with the ultimate goal: **by 2050, a fully autonomous humanoid robot soccer team will defeat the human World Cup champions**.

2026 marks the first time RoboCup is held in **South Korea**, expecting **3,000+ participants** from dozens of countries and approximately **15,000 spectators**.

### Basic Info

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Date** | June 30 - July 6, 2026 |
| **Location** | Songdo Convensia, Incheon, South Korea |
| **Organizers** | Incheon Metropolitan City, Korea Association of AI Robot Industry (KAR), Incheon Technopark (ITP) |
| **Website** | https://2026.robocup.org |
| **Scale** | 3,000+ participants, 15,000+ spectators |
| **History** | 30th edition, first time in South Korea |
| **Next Edition** | Nuremberg, Germany in 2027 |

### League System (Five Major Leagues)

| League | Sub-events |
|--------|------------|
| **RoboCupSoccer** | Standard Platform (NAO), Humanoid (KidSize/AdultSize), Mid-size, Small-Size, Simulation 2D, Simulation 3D |
| **RoboCupRescue** | Rescue Robot (physical search in simulated debris), Rescue Simulation |
| **RoboCup@Home** | Domestic service robots, social robots, autonomous navigation & manipulation |
| **RoboCupIndustrial** | Industrial mobile manipulation |
| **RoboCupJunior** | K-12 robotics competition |

### Awards

- League champions, runners-up, third places
- RoboCup Symposium Best Paper Award
- Silvia Coradeschi RoboCup Award

## 7. RoboSub 2026 (29th AUV Competition)

### Overview

RoboSub is the **world's most influential autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV) competition**, founded in 1997 with venue support from the U.S. Navy. Open to high school and university teams, it challenges participants to design and build fully autonomous underwater robots for complex navigation, object recognition, and manipulation tasks. The 2026 theme is **"Ocean Exploration & Mapping"**.

### Basic Info

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Date** | July 11 - 16, 2026 |
| **Location** | Woollett Aquatics Center, Irvine, CA, USA |
| **Organizer** | RoboNation / U.S. Navy |
| **Website** | https://robosub.org/programs/2026/ |
| **Edition** | 29th |
| **Eligibility** | High school and university teams |
| **Registration Fee** | $1,500/team |

### Mission Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Validation Gate | Demonstrate basic propulsion and depth control |
| Buoy Interaction | Detect and touch a buoy of a specified color |
| Marker Deployment | Drop markers into a target bin |
| Acoustic Beacon Localization | Use hydrophone array to locate underwater acoustic signals |
| Torpedo Launch | Fire torpedoes through targets |
| Pipe Recognition & Tracking | Simulate subsea infrastructure inspection |

### Technical Specifications

- **Autonomy**: No wired remote control or teleoperation allowed
- **Weight Limit**: ~125 lbs
- **Safety**: Emergency kill switch (internal + external)
- **Depth**: 14-16 ft
- **Recommended Sensors**: IMU, pressure sensor, DVL, hydrophones, camera
