## 1. ICRA 2026 — IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation

### Overview

ICRA is the **largest and most influential flagship conference** in robotics, organized by the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS). It is classified as a **CCF B-class** conference by the China Computer Federation. ICRA covers all areas of robotics and serves as a premier venue for embodied AI papers (VLA, manipulation, navigation, humanoid robots, etc.).

The 2026 theme is **"Robots for All"**, held for the first time in Vienna, Austria, with thousands of researchers expected. The conference also hosts **9 robotics competitions** (including the AgiBot World Challenge for embodied AI), making it one of the most practice-oriented robotics conferences.

### Key Information

| Item | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **Dates** | June 1-5, 2026 |
| **Location** | Vienna, Austria · VIECON / Messe Wien (Messeplatz 1, 1020 Vienna) |
| **Organizer** | IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (IEEE RAS) |
| **Official Website** | https://2026.ieee-icra.org/ |
| **CCF Rank** | B-class |
| **Edition** | 43rd |
| **Theme** | Robots for All |
| **Paper Submission** | Closed (deadline Jan 2026) |

### Core Topics

| Area | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Robot Manipulation & Grasping** | Dexterous manipulation, bimanual coordination, object picking and placement |
| **Motion Planning & Control** | Whole-body control, legged/humanoid locomotion, model predictive control |
| **Perception & Navigation** | SLAM, 3D scene understanding, autonomous navigation |
| **Human-Robot Interaction** | Physical HRI, social robotics, teleoperation |
| **Robot Learning** | Reinforcement learning, imitation learning, VLA models, world models |
| **Multi-Robot Systems** | Swarm coordination, task allocation, distributed control |

### 2026 Competitions

ICRA 2026 hosts 9 robotics competitions, with the following directly relevant to embodied AI:

- AgiBot World Challenge 2026 (embodied humanoid robots: World Model / VLM+VLA / Whole-Body Control tracks)
- REAL-I Real-World Embodied Intelligence Learning Challenge (3 industrial scenarios)
- 11th Robot Grasping and Manipulation Competition
- What Bimanuals Can Do competition
- LeHome Challenge 2026 (home scene cloth manipulation)

## 2. IROS 2026 — IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems

### Overview

IROS is the **second-largest flagship robotics conference**, on par with ICRA, jointly organized by IEEE RAS and RSJ (Robotics Society of Japan). It is classified as a **CCF C-class** conference. IROS emphasizes **intelligent robotic system integration and innovation**, covering the full stack from sensors to decision-making.

In 2026, IROS returns to Pittsburgh, USA — home to Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) and a global hub for robotics research and autonomous vehicle companies. IROS 2026 is expected to receive 1,000+ papers.

<a name="3-rss-2026-robotics-science-and-systems"></a>

## 3. RSS 2026 — Robotics: Science and Systems

### Overview

RSS is **one of the three top robotics conferences** (alongside ICRA and IROS), renowned for **exceptionally high paper quality and low acceptance rate**. It is classified as a **CCF B-class** conference. RSS focuses on foundational theory and systemic innovation in robotics, accepting only about 100-150 papers per edition, making it the premier platform for breakthrough work.

The 22nd edition is held for the **first time in Sydney, Australia**, hosted by the University of Technology Sydney (UTS).

### Key Information

| Item | Details |
|:---|:---|
| **Dates** | July 13-17, 2026 |
| **Location** | Sydney, Australia · UTS / International Convention Centre Sydney |
| **Organizer** | Robotics Foundation |
| **Official Website** | https://roboticsconference.org/ |
| **CCF Rank** | B-class |
| **Edition** | 22nd |
| **Abstract Deadline** | January 24, 2026 (closed) |
| **Paper Submission Deadline** | January 31, 2026 (closed) |

### Core Features

- **Single Track**: All accepted papers are presented as oral talks, no poster sessions
- **Pre-Publication Review**: All accepted papers are published online before the conference
- **High Selectivity**: Annual acceptance rate of ~25-30%, significantly lower than ICRA/IROS
- **Rich Workshops**: Hosts numerous highly interactive workshops, a hub for frontier discussions (embodied AI, world models, etc.)

### Connection to Embodied AI

RSS has recently accepted a large number of embodied AI papers, including:

- Vision-based robot manipulation (VLA models)
- World models for robotics
- Whole-body control of humanoid robots
- New paradigms for imitation learning and teaching from demonstration

## 4. CoRL 2026 — Conference on Robot Learning

### Overview

CoRL is a **top-tier conference at the intersection of robotics and machine learning**, dedicated to cutting-edge research in robot learning including reinforcement learning, imitation learning, and VLA models. It has rapidly become a **core venue for embodied AI publications** — milestone works such as OpenVLA and RT-2 were first presented at CoRL.

The 10th edition covers the full pipeline of robot learning, from algorithms to real-world deployment. The conference features 1 day of Workshops + 3 days of main conference.

| **CCF Rank** | Not officially ranked (emerging top conference, rapidly growing influence) |
| **Edition** | 10th |

### Core Topics

| Area | Description |
|:---|:---|
| **Robot Manipulation Learning** | Dexterous manipulation, object grasping, out-of-the-box skill learning |
| **Imitation Learning** | Behavioral cloning, inverse reinforcement learning, demonstration-based policy learning |
| **Reinforcement Learning** | Deep RL, offline RL, model-based RL |
| **VLA & Multimodal** | Vision-Language-Action models, large model-driven robot policies |
| **Navigation & Exploration** | Autonomous exploration, goal-driven navigation learning |

### Connection to Embodied AI

CoRL is **one of the most active venues in embodied AI**, with 2025-2026 papers heavily featuring:

- OpenVLA and its variants

- World models in robotic manipulation

## 9. IEEE-RAS Humanoids 2026 — IEEE-RAS International Conference on Humanoid Robots

The 25th edition (Silver Jubilee) is held in **Silicon Valley**, closely tied to the global humanoid robotics industry hub. Expected attendance: 1,200+ with 200+ paper submissions.
